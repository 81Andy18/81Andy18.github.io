iOS

  iOS

iOS is a mobile operating system developed by Apple. Apple first announced this system at the Macworld conference on January 9, 2007. It was originally designed for use on the iPhone, and later applied to the iPod touch and iPad. Like Apple's macOS operating system, iOS is a Unix-like commercial operating system. Originally this system was called iPhone OS. Because iPad, iPhone, and iPod touch all use iPhone OS, it was announced on WWDC in 2010 that it was renamed iOS (iOS is a registered trademark of Cisco’s network equipment operating system in the United States, and the rename of Apple has been authorized by Cisco) .

In January 2016, with the release of iOS 9.2.1, Apple fixed a vulnerability that had existed for 3 years. On September 22, 2018, Apple in the United States secretly added a "trust rating" function based on iPhone users and users of other devices of the company in the latest operating system.

On November 17, 2021, according to a 9to5 Mac report, Apple’s iOS 15.2 Beta leaked the new "SportsKit" framework.
